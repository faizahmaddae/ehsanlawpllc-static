Every immigration case is unique. Here are the areas where we help clients most often — with compassion, expertise, and a commitment to justice.

Helping You Find Safety and Hope

Asylum

Applying for asylum can be one of the most difficult and emotional steps in your life. Our firm has represented hundreds of asylum seekers from Afghanistan and around the world — both affirmative asylum applications (USCIS) and defensive asylum cases in immigration court.

Every asylum story matters. We'll help make sure yours is heard and understood.

We help you:

  • Prepare a powerful and credible personal declaration
  • Gather and organize supporting evidence and country condition reports
  • Get ready for your asylum interview or court testimony
  • File withholding of removal or Convention Against Torture (CAT) claims, if eligible

Protecting Your Right to Stay

Removal & Deportation Defense

If you or a loved one is facing deportation or in immigration detention, every decision matters. Our attorneys handle cases before immigration courts across the United States — including detained clients in facilities such as Tacoma, WA, and others nationwide.

We'll fight for your right to stay in the U.S. and keep your life and family intact.

We build strong, compassionate defenses for:

  • Detained immigrants in ICE custody
  • Cancellation of Removal (EOIR-42A/B) cases
  • Bond hearings and custody redeterminations
  • Motions to reopen and reconsider
  • Appeals to the Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA)

Fighting for Justice at Every Level

BIA & Federal Court Appeals

If the Immigration Judge has ordered you removed or denied your case, you still have hope. The Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA) reviews decisions made by immigration courts across the country — and a well-prepared appeal can make all the difference.

When the BIA denies your case, the U.S. Court of Appeals is your final chance to seek justice. Our team combines legal precision with powerful storytelling.

We handle:

  • Appeals of denied asylum and withholding cases
  • Challenges to deportation and removal orders
  • Bond redetermination appeals
  • Motions to reopen or reconsider BIA decisions
  • Petitions for Review of BIA decisions
  • Federal appeals involving asylum, cancellation, and removal orders
  • Emergency stays of removal
⚠️ Important: Federal appeals are complex and time-sensitive. If your case was denied by the BIA, you typically have 30 days to file a petition for review.

Holding the Government Accountable

Mandamus Lawsuits & Delay Challenges

Many immigrants wait months — sometimes years — for USCIS to make a decision on their application. If you've submitted everything and the government still hasn't acted, a federal mandamus lawsuit can force them to respond.

We hold the government accountable — so you can move forward with your life.

We help clients nationwide by filing federal actions under the Administrative Procedure Act (APA) and Mandamus Act to compel timely decisions for:

  • Asylum, Green Card, or Citizenship applications
  • I-601 and I-601A waivers
  • SIV and humanitarian parole cases
  • Long-pending work permits and status adjustments
